LS One POS: Best Price

When an item is sold in LS One POS the POS has to find the correct price for the item, this is called the item’s best price.

The POS looks for the item’s sales price in following places:

  • Base sales price on the retail item card.
  • The sales price in Trade agreements.
  • Promotion price.

When an item is sold on the POS, the POS:

  • Starts by finding the Base sales price.
  • Secondly, it looks for the Trade agreements sales price and Promotion price.
  • Thirdly the POS has rules to decide which price will be the item’s sales line best price.

The best price is found by using the following rules:

  • The Base sales price is always overruled by the Trade agreement sales price even though the Base sales price is lower.
  • The lowest promotion price is used only when it is lower than the Base sales price and Trade agreement sales price.
  • When a sales price exists in Trade agreements and the Promotion price is the lower of these, the Promotion price is used.
  • When many Trade agreement sales prices or promotion prices are found, the lowest of them is used for the best price comparison.

The best price for the item will be the item’s sales price in the POS.

The following example table explains how the best price for an item is found:

  Price
Base Price 85 85 85 85 85 85 85 85
Trade agreements sales price 90 80 - - 90 90 80 80
Promotion price - - 110 80 105 75 105 75
Best price = POS Price 90 80 85 80 90 75 80 75
