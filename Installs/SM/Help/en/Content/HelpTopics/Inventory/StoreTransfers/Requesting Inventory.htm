Requesting Inventory

The Low inventory store (see example) just noticed that it is running low on bananas and apples. They call their main office to see if any of their other stores has a surplus of fruit that they can have. The main office tells them that the High inventory store has too much fruit available and tells them to create an inventory transfer request directed at High inventory store for the fruit they require.

The Low inventory store goes to the Site Manager and creates the inventory transfer request. They include a request of 100 kg of bananas and 50 kg of apples.

Image: An unsent inventory transfer request for some fruit

They send the request and 30 seconds later call the High inventory store to ask if they saw the inventory transfer request that is waiting for them.

The High inventory store had seen the request and the stock room is already checking if they can send the requested amounts.

Image: The High inventory store create an inventory transfer order

The High inventory store has enough inventory in stock to fulfill the Low inventory store’s request.

The next step is for the high inventory store to create an inventory transfer order based on the request.
