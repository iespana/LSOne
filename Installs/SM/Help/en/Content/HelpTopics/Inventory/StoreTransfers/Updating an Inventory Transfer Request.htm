Updating an Inventory Transfer Request

The Low inventory store is running low on bananas for the third week in a row. They will have to deal with their ordering process later but right now they need some bananas before they run out.

As in the three previous weeks they call their friends at the High inventory store and ask them if they have some bananas to spare. And again, they do have some spare bananas to send them. The Low inventory store sends the High inventory store a request.

See Overview for further explanation of this example.

Image: A Transfer Request Sent but not Fetched

The Fetched checkbox has not been selected

 

After sending an inventory transfer request for 100 kg of bananas the manager of the Low inventory store decides to go on a lunch break. But before he can finish his sandwich one of his employees come running to him telling him that they are also running low on apples and will require at least 50 kg before the end of the day.

The manager quickly opens the Site Manager again and checks if the inventory transfer request he just sent has been fetched by the High inventory store. He knows that until that happens he can add to the inventory transfer request. He sees that it has not been fetched so he adds a line for 50 kg of apples to the request and resends it. He knows that if the High inventory store has some extra apples they will include it in the inventory transfer order.
